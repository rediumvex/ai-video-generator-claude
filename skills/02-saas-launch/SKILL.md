## The SaaS Video Problem

Most SaaS videos are screen recordings with a voiceover. They look like tutorials, not launches. The fix: treat your product like a character in a film. Give it lighting, movement, depth, and emotion.

### The Three Laws of SaaS Video

1. **Never show a flat screenshot.** Always add depth — perspective, reflection, environment, motion.
2. **The UI is the hero, not the narrator.** Show the interface doing things, not someone explaining it.
3. **Sound sells more than visuals.** A satisfying click, a smooth transition sound, a bass hit on feature reveal — sound makes software feel real.

## UI Cinematography — Making Software Beautiful

### The Floating Device Shot

The most versatile SaaS video technique. Product shown on a device floating in stylized 3D space.

```
FLOATING DEVICE TEMPLATE:

"MacBook/phone/tablet floating in dark void at slight 15-degree angle.
Screen displays [product UI — reference @image1]. Device has subtle
reflection on invisible surface below. Single key light from upper-left
at 45 degrees creates soft shadow. Rim light from behind separates device
from black background.

Camera slowly orbits 30 degrees over [duration]. UI on screen is live —
elements animate, data flows, cursors move. Screen glow illuminates
immediate surrounding space with product's brand color.

Sound: minimal ambient electronic. UI interaction sounds (clicks, swooshes)
synced to on-screen activity. Clean, modern, premium."
```

### The Perspective Zoom

Camera pushes INTO the screen, transitioning from physical device to full-screen UI.

```
PERSPECTIVE ZOOM TEMPLATE:

"Start with wide shot: device on desk in stylish workspace. Warm ambient
lighting, shallow depth of field. Camera begins pushing toward screen.

At 2s: camera crosses the screen boundary — transition from physical
space to digital space. The UI fills the entire frame. Camera continues
moving forward, navigating through interface elements as if flying
through the product.

UI elements pass by camera at different depths — parallax effect.
Sidebar slides past on left, cards float by on right. Navigation
elements form a corridor. Camera moves through the product like
walking through a building.

Sound: real-world ambient fades out as camera enters screen.
Digital ambient fades in — clean, processed, reverb-free. UI sounds
become prominent. Music becomes more electronic/digital."
```

### The Split Comparison

Before/after or competitor comparison that makes your product the obvious winner.

```
SPLIT COMPARISON TEMPLATE:

"Frame divided vertically or horizontally. Left/top: competitor product
or old workflow — cluttered, confusing, many clicks. Flat lighting,
slightly desaturated. Right/bottom: your product — clean, minimal,
one-click solution. Premium lighting, full color.

Both sides animate simultaneously. Left side shows complex multi-step
process. Right side shows single elegant interaction achieving same
result. Timer on each side counts the steps/seconds.

At resolution: left side still processing. Right side: done. Checkmark
animation. Confetti or subtle celebration effect.

Sound: left side has frustrated, cluttered audio (notifications, errors).
Right side has clean, satisfying clicks and completion sounds."
```

## Lighting for Software

### Dark Mode Showcase
```
"Product displayed on device with dark-mode UI. Environment is dark room.
Screen is primary light source — UI colors illuminate surroundings.
Brand accent color creates colored light spill on desk surface and walls.
Subtle volumetric haze makes light rays from screen visible.
No other light sources. Screen glow is hero. Moody, premium, modern."
```

### Clean Studio
```
"Product on device on white/light gray surface. Soft diffused overhead
lighting — no harsh shadows. Fill light from all directions creating
minimal shadow. Background is clean gradient (white to light gray).
Product packaging or brand elements positioned as props.
Sound: soft ambient, no music, focus on UI click sounds.
Apple-style product photography as video."
```

### Neon Tech
```
"Dark environment. Neon accent lighting in brand colors positioned as
edge lights and rim lights. Device floating or on matte black surface.
Neon reflections on device surface and screen bezels. Slight fog/haze
for volumetric light effect. Cyberpunk-adjacent but professional.
UI glow combines with neon ambient for unified color story."
```

## Sound Design for SaaS

### The Interaction Sound Library

Every UI interaction needs a sound. These make software feel tangible.

| UI Action | Sound | Prompt Phrasing |
|---|---|---|
| Button click | Crisp tactile tap | "Soft mechanical click — like premium keyboard key. Short, clean, satisfying. No reverb." |
| Page transition | Smooth swoosh | "Gentle horizontal swoosh, 0.3s duration. Soft air movement sound. Stereo pan matching transition direction." |
| Data loading | Digital cascade | "Quick digital cascade — ascending pitched clicks like data packets arriving. 0.5s total. Creates feeling of speed." |
| Success/complete | Confirmation tone | "Two-note ascending chime. Clean sine wave. Major interval. Short decay. Universally signals 'done, success.'" |
| Error/warning | Soft alert | "Single low-pitched tone. Gentle, not alarming. 0.2s duration. Communicates 'attention needed' without frustration." |
| Dashboard populate | Data waterfall | "Rapid series of soft clicks at varying pitches — like rain on glass. Represents data points appearing. 1s total." |

### Music for SaaS Videos

```
"Background music: minimal electronic, clean production. No vocals.
BPM 90-110 (calm confidence, not frantic). Subtle bass foundation.
Synth pads for atmosphere. Light percussion — hi-hats, snaps, not
heavy drums. The music should feel like the product sounds: clean,
modern, efficient. Key moments (feature reveals, transitions) get
a slight musical emphasis — filter sweep or bass drop."
```

## Prompt Rules for SaaS

1. **Always show the product doing something.** Static screenshots are death. Animate everything.
2. **Use real UI references.** Upload actual product screenshots as `@image1`. The AI should incorporate your actual interface.
3. **Sound is mandatory.** Every click, transition, and data event needs audio. Software without sound feels fake.
4. **Match your brand lighting.** Dark-mode product → dark environment. Light-mode → clean studio. The physical environment should echo the digital one.
5. **End on the product.** Last frame should be your product in its best light, not a generic call-to-action.
